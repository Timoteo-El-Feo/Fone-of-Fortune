What Is This?

You're looking at a restored AT&T Traditional 1000 telephone from 1986 — just like one that my family owned when I was in elementary school.

This phone no longer connects to the wall by our old writing desk, however. It has been rewired, reprogrammed, and reborn as something stranger: a fortune-telling machine.

Lift the handset. The phone will speak to you — a recorded fortune, drawn at random from a collection of voices, wisdom, and carefully curated nonsense culled from over 300 actual fortunes I've collected over a 30-year span. Each call is different. Each fortune is yours alone. But wait, there's more! Can you find the secrets within?

About the Object

"We used to trust the telephone to carry the most important moments of our lives — love, loss, friendship, emergency. What would it mean to trust it with something more uncertain: the future?"

— Artist's Statement

The exterior of the phone is embedded with the actual fortunes from fortune cookies I've ecstatically devoured, sealed under layers of protective epoxy — a palimpsest of accumulated wisdom, superstition, lucky lottery numbers, and the odd Chinese lesson.

Inside lives a small computer no larger than a credit card, an audio amplifier, a speaker, and a MicroSD card with eight gigabytes of recorded voices summoned from beyond. The old wiring has been replaced; the new wiring asks different questions.

Instructions & Controls

The Fone of Fortune is not complicated — but like all good oracles, it rewards a little curiosity. Here is what the various sliders, buttons, and hidden switches actually do.

Control

Handset

Lift the handset and place the receiver to your ear to hear your fortune. The oracle stirs after a brief pause — it is gathering itself.

Replace the handset in the cradle when you are finished. This resets the Fone and returns the oracle to its slumber, ready for the next caller.

📍 The receiver · resting in the cradle at the top of the phone
Control

Receiver Vol

The slider on the lower left of the keypad controls the listening volume. Slide it to the right to turn up the oracle; slide it left to bring things down to a whisper.

There is no wrong volume. Though the oracle does seem to prefer being heard clearly.

📍 Lower left · below the number pad
Control

Ringer Off · Lo · Hi

The switch on the lower right of the keypad is the phone's main power and audio routing switch. Think of it as the bouncer at the door of the oracle's chamber.

OFF — The oracle sleeps. The phone rests between callers.
LO — The oracle speaks through the handset earpiece. A private consultation, for your ears only.
HI — The oracle addresses the room. Audio routes through the speaker in the base of the phone.

📍 Lower right · below the number pad
Hidden

The Hidden Mode Switch

Gently flip the phone over. On the underside of the base, you will find a small DIAL switch marked DP · TT. This is the oracle's hidden mode selector — a relic of the phone's original purpose, quietly repurposed for stranger things.

DP (dial pulse) — Shuffle mode. Lift the handset; receive a fortune. Simple, direct, reliable — like a good horoscope.
TT (touch tone) — Advanced mode. Lift the handset, then dial. The keypad awakens. What you dial determines what you receive.

📍 Underside of the phone base · small DIAL toggle switch
Advanced

The Keypad

In Shuffle mode, the keypad rests quietly. In Advanced mode — with the hidden DIAL switch set to TT — the keypad becomes a portal to something deeper.

To place a call, dial your number or code, then press # to send it. Think of # as the old-fashioned way of saying "I'm ready, patch me through." Try numbers that feel meaningful, symbolic, or that you are drawn to. Not every combination will connect — but some will surprise you.

The function buttons to the right of the number pad add another dimension to the Fone of Fortune.

MUTE — toggles sound output on and off.
RE DIAL — plays the last played fortune again.
MEM — plays a fortune PROGrammed into a folder tied to a MEM slot.
PROG — programs the last played fortune into a slot number you dial. For example, PROG 1# stores the last played fortune to the MEM 1 slot. To recall that same fortune at any time, dial MEM 1#.
