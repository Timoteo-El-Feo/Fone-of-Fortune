What Is This?

You're looking at a restored AT&T Traditional 1000 telephone from 1986 — one that my family actually owned when I was in elementary school.

But this phone no longer connects to the wall by our old writing desk. It has been rewired, reprogrammed, and reborn as something stranger: a fortune-telling machine.

Lift the handset. The phone will speak to you — a recorded fortune, drawn at random from a collection of voices, wisdom, and carefully curated nonsense culled from actual fortunes I had collected from the 90s onwards. Each call is different. Each fortune is yours alone.

🥠
How to Consult the Oracle
  1. I
    Lift the handset. The phone will stir to life. A brief pause — the oracle is consulting the ether.
  2. II
    Listen. Your fortune will be delivered through the earpiece. Hold it close; oracles don't repeat themselves.
  3. III
    Replace the handset when you are finished. The oracle returns to its slumber, awaiting the next seeker.
  4. IV
    Optional: Dial the keypad for deeper mysteries. Not all of them are ready for you yet. Or should I say, you may not be ready for all of them yet.

About the Object

"We used to trust the telephone to carry the most important moments of our lives — birth, grief, love, emergency. What would it mean to trust it with something more uncertain: the future?"

— Artist's Statement

The exterior of the phone is embedded with actual fortune cookie slips, sealed under layers of protective varnish — a palimpsest of accumulated wisdom, superstition, lucky lottery numbers, and the odd Chinese lesson.

Inside lives a small computer no larger than a credit card, an audio amplifier, a speaker, and eight gigabytes of recorded voices. The old wiring has been replaced; the new wiring asks different questions.
